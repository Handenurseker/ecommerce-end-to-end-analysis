## Business Problem
This project aims to analyze e-commerce sales data by examining total revenue, city-level performance, category-based sales distribution, and customer behavior. 
Using SQL and Power BI, key performance indicators were analyzed, high-performing cities and customers were identified, and visual reports were created to support business decision-making.
## Analysis Questions
- What is the total revenue generated from e-commerce sales?
- Which cities generate the highest revenue?
- Which customers contribute the most to overall revenue?
- What is the average revenue per customer?
- Which cities perform above average in sales?
- What sales trends can be observed over time?
- How does customer purchasing behavior differ across customers?

## Key Insights
- Certain cities outperform others in both total revenue and customer concentration.
- Some cities generate above-average sales, indicating stronger market potential.
- Customer revenue contribution is unevenly distributed, highlighting high-value customer groups.
- Sales performance varies across dimensions such as city and customer, which can help prioritize business focus.

## Business Recommendations
- Focus marketing efforts on top-performing cities to maximize revenue growth.
- Develop retention strategies for high-value customers who contribute significantly to total revenue.
- Investigate underperforming cities to identify opportunities for improvement.
- Use customer contribution analysis to design targeted campaigns for different customer groups.
- Monitor sales trends regularly to support faster business decisions.
